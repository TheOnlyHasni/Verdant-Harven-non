Shopping Guides

Shop smarter, not harder. Our guides help you find quality and value for your home and garden.

Shop Your Own Home First

Before buying anything new, take inventory of what you already own. Moving a lamp from the bedroom to the living room or rearranging artwork can give a space a completely fresh feel at no cost.

The Power of Paint

Paint is the most affordable and impactful way to transform a room. Consider painting an accent wall, updating old furniture with a fresh coat, or even painting outdated floor tiles.

Master the Art of Thrifting

Secondhand stores are treasure troves for unique, high-quality items. Look for solid wood furniture, unique glassware, and picture frames. These pieces often have more character than brand-new items.

Splurge: High-Traffic Furniture

Invest in pieces that get the most use, like your sofa, mattress, and dining table. A well-made frame and quality upholstery will last for years, saving you money in the long run. Look for solid wood construction and durable fabrics.

Save: Trendy Decor & Accessories

Pillows, throws, vases, and small decorative objects are perfect places to save. These items can be easily and affordably swapped out as your style evolves or seasons change. Look for these at thrift stores or budget-friendly retailers.

Splurge: Classic Lighting

A beautiful, timeless light fixture can act as the "jewelry" of a room. Investing in a quality chandelier, pendant, or floor lamp can elevate the entire space and will remain stylish for decades.

Save: Artwork

You don't need to spend a fortune on art. Frame personal photos, create your own abstract paintings, or find affordable prints online. A large, inexpensive frame can make any piece look high-end.
